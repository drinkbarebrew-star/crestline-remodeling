Bathroom Remodeling Cost in Naperville (2026 Guide)

June 30, 2026 | Crestline Remodeling Team

A bathroom remodel is one of the highest-impact upgrades you can make to a Naperville home. It is the room you start and end every day in, and a dated, cramped, or worn-out bathroom drags down the feel of the whole house. It is also one of the most reliable renovations for resale value in the western suburbs. But before you start pulling tile samples, you need a realistic budget. This guide breaks down 2026 bathroom remodeling costs in Naperville, what drives the numbers, and how to plan a project that delivers.

Average Bathroom Remodel Cost in Naperville (2026)

As of 2026, most Naperville homeowners spend between $12,000 and $35,000 on a bathroom remodel. That is a wide range because "bathroom remodel" covers everything from swapping a vanity and re-tiling a tub surround to gutting a primary suite and reconfiguring the entire layout. Where your project lands depends on the size of the space, the quality of materials, and how much you change the existing footprint.

Here is how the three most common tiers break down for a typical Naperville bathroom.

Remodel Tier Typical Cost What's Included
Budget refresh $9,000 – $15,000 New vanity, toilet, fixtures, paint, re-tiled tub surround, same layout
Mid-range full remodel $18,000 – $30,000 New tile floor and walls, custom vanity, quartz top, updated lighting and plumbing fixtures
Luxury / primary suite $35,000 – $60,000+ Layout changes, walk-in shower with frameless glass, double vanity, heated floors, high-end finishes

What Drives Bathroom Remodel Costs

Two bathrooms of the same size can have very different price tags. These are the factors that move the budget most.

1. Size and Layout Changes

A powder room costs far less than a primary bath simply because there is less to do. But the bigger cost driver is whether you move anything. Keeping the toilet, sink, and shower in their existing locations is the single most effective way to control the budget. Relocating plumbing, moving a wall to expand the footprint, or converting a tub to a curbless walk-in shower all add significant labor and materials.

2. Materials and Finishes

Material choices account for a huge swing in price. Porcelain tile, a stock vanity, and a standard quartz top keep costs reasonable, while natural stone, custom cabinetry, designer fixtures, and frameless glass enclosures push toward the luxury tier. The good news is that a well-chosen mix, splurging on a statement floor tile while keeping the layout and plumbing fixtures sensible, delivers a high-end look without a luxury budget.

3. Plumbing and Electrical

Older Naperville homes sometimes hide outdated galvanized plumbing or under-capacity wiring behind the walls. Once the room is opened up, bringing these up to current code adds cost but is money well spent. Adding new circuits for heated floors, additional lighting, or an exhaust fan also factors in.

4. Tile and Labor

Tile work is one of the most labor-intensive parts of any bathroom and one of the most visible. Intricate patterns, large-format tile, niches, and full-height shower walls all take skilled time to install correctly and waterproof. Quality tile labor is where a bathroom either looks custom or looks rushed.

5. Permits and Inspections

Any remodel involving plumbing, electrical, or structural changes requires permits and inspections in Naperville. A licensed contractor handles this as part of the project, ensuring the work is done to code and protecting you at resale.

Where to Spend and Where to Save

The smartest bathroom budgets concentrate money where it shows and lasts. Spend on the items that are hard or disruptive to change later: waterproofing, plumbing rough-in, quality tile labor, and a durable, well-built vanity. These are the bones of the bathroom, and cutting corners here leads to callbacks and early failure.

Save on items that are easy to swap and where the price gap does not buy much durability: light fixtures, mirrors, cabinet hardware, and paint. You can also save meaningfully by keeping the existing layout, choosing a beautiful but mid-priced porcelain tile over natural stone, and selecting a stock or semi-custom vanity instead of fully custom millwork. Pairing a bathroom project with new flooring installation elsewhere in the home can also reduce per-project overhead.

Does a Bathroom Remodel Add Value?

Bathrooms and kitchens are the two rooms that most influence a home's sale price in the Naperville market. A mid-range bathroom remodel typically returns a strong share of its cost at resale, and just as importantly, an updated bathroom helps a home sell faster. Buyers notice dated bathrooms immediately, and a clean, modern space removes a common objection. Even if you are not selling soon, a remodel you enjoy every day is a worthwhile investment in how your home functions.

How Long Does a Bathroom Remodel Take?

A typical full bathroom remodel in Naperville takes three to five weeks from demolition to final walkthrough. The schedule runs roughly like this: demolition and rough-in plumbing and electrical in the first week, inspections and waterproofing next, then tile, vanity, fixtures, and finish work. Layout changes, permit review, and special-order materials such as custom vanities or imported tile can extend the timeline, which is why ordering materials early is part of good project planning.

Why Work With a Local Naperville Contractor

Bathrooms are unforgiving. Water and improper waterproofing cause expensive damage when the work is done poorly, so this is not the place for an unlicensed handyman or a weekend DIY gut job. A licensed, insured local remodeler knows Naperville permit requirements, coordinates the plumbers, electricians, and tile setters, and stands behind the waterproofing that keeps the room sound for decades. Working with an established bathroom remodeling team also means accurate budgeting up front and fewer surprises once the walls are open.
