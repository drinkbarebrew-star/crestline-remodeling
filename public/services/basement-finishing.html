Basement Finishing

North Shore Basement Finishing You Can Count On

A finished basement is the most cost-effective square footage you can add to a North Shore home — no new foundation, no roofline changes. Crestline Remodeling finishes basements with proper moisture management, egress, and insulation so the space stays comfortable and dry year-round.

We’ve finished basements as media rooms, home gyms, wine cellars, guest suites, and full in-law apartments, adding an average of 900–1,400 square feet of usable living space.

Quick stat: 1,200 — sq ft added on average.

What’s Included

  • Moisture mitigation, sump systems, and vapor barriers
  • Egress window installation for code-compliant bedrooms
  • Home theaters, gyms, playrooms, and wet bars
  • Full in-law and au pair suites with kitchenettes
  • Spray-foam and rigid insulation for year-round comfort
  • Bathroom additions with ejector pump systems

What Does Basement Finishing Cost in Highland Park?

A basic finished basement runs $35,000–$55,000; adding a bathroom, wet bar, or in-law suite pushes projects to $75,000–$110,000+. Every project is different, so Crestline provides a fixed-price proposal after the design phase — no vague ranges, no surprise change orders.

Typical investment: $35,000 – $110,000 for basement finishing on the North Shore.

Our Process

Basement Finishing FAQs

Common questions about basement finishing on Chicago’s North Shore.

Finishing a basement in Highland Park typically costs $35,000 to $110,000 depending on scope. A straightforward finish with a rec room and storage runs $35,000–$55,000; adding a full bathroom, wet bar, or in-law suite with a kitchenette brings the total to $75,000–$110,000+. Moisture control and egress are non-negotiable line items we never skip.
If your finished basement will include a bedroom, Illinois code requires a compliant egress window for emergency exit. Even when not required, we recommend egress windows for the natural light and resale value they add. Crestline handles the excavation, window well, and permitting as part of the project.
We start with the water before the walls: assessing grading, sump performance, and any past seepage, then installing vapor barriers, proper insulation, and dehumidification. On the North Shore’s clay soils this step is critical. We won’t finish a basement we can’t keep dry, and we stand behind the moisture work in writing.
