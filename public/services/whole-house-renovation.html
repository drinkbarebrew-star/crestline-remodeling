Whole-House Renovation

North Shore Whole-House Renovation You Can Count On

The North Shore is full of beautiful, character-rich homes with layouts that no longer fit modern life. A whole-house renovation lets you keep the address, the trees, and the architecture while rebuilding the way the home functions from the inside out.

Crestline Remodeling acts as your single point of accountability across architecture, structural engineering, mechanical systems, and finishes. We’ve renovated homes from 1920s Highland Park Tudors to mid-century Glencoe ranches, modernizing systems while preserving the details that give these homes their value.

Quick stat: 100% — single-team accountability.

What’s Included

  • Full architectural redesign and space planning
  • Kitchen, bathroom, and living-space reconfiguration
  • Electrical, plumbing, and HVAC system replacement
  • Window, insulation, and energy-efficiency upgrades
  • Historic detail preservation and restoration
  • Whole-home flooring, millwork, and finish packages

What Does Whole-House Renovation Cost in Highland Park?

Whole-house renovations on the North Shore typically run $250,000–$900,000+, or roughly $150–$350 per square foot depending on finish level and structural scope. Every project is different, so Crestline provides a fixed-price proposal after the design phase — no vague ranges, no surprise change orders.

Typical investment: $250,000 – $900,000 for whole-house renovation on the North Shore.

Our Process

  1. Free consultation — we visit your home, listen to your goals, and discuss a realistic budget range.
  2. Design & engineering — floor plans, 3D renderings, and material selections with real-time pricing.
  3. Fixed-price proposal — a detailed, line-item contract you approve before any work begins.
  4. Construction — a dedicated project manager, clean job sites, and weekly progress updates.
  5. Final walkthrough & warranty — we don’t finish until you’re thrilled, and we stand behind our work.
