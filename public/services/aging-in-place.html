Aging-in-Place Remodeling

North Shore Aging-in-Place Remodeling You Can Count On

Many North Shore homeowners want to stay in their homes as they age, but homes built decades ago weren’t designed for it. Crestline Remodeling specializes in aging-in-place and universal design renovations that add safety and accessibility without making a home feel clinical.

We hold the Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist (CAPS) credential and design modifications that look like intentional upgrades — curbless showers, wider doorways, and first-floor suites — so homes remain beautiful and become barrier-free.

Quick stat: CAPS — certified aging-in-place specialists.

What’s Included

  • Curbless, roll-in walk-in showers with grab bars
  • First-floor primary suite conversions
  • Widened doorways and hallways for mobility
  • Zero-step entries and exterior ramps
  • Comfort-height fixtures and accessible cabinetry
  • Improved lighting and slip-resistant flooring

What Does Aging-in-Place Remodeling Cost in Highland Park?

Targeted modifications like an accessible bathroom run $15,000–$35,000; a full first-floor suite conversion can run $60,000–$90,000+. Every project is different, so Crestline provides a fixed-price proposal after the design phase — no vague ranges, no surprise change orders.

Typical investment: $10,000 – $90,000 for aging-in-place remodeling on the North Shore.

Our Process

  1. Free consultation — we visit your home, listen to your goals, and discuss a realistic budget range.
  2. Design & engineering — floor plans, 3D renderings, and material selections with real-time pricing.
  3. Fixed-price proposal — a detailed, line-item contract you approve before any work begins.
  4. Construction — a dedicated project manager, clean job sites, and weekly progress updates.
  5. Final walkthrough & warranty — we don’t finish until you’re thrilled, and we stand behind our work.

Aging-in-Place Remodeling FAQs

Common questions about aging-in-place remodeling on Chicago’s North Shore.

Aging-in-place projects in Highland Park typically run $10,000 to $90,000+ depending on scope. A single accessible bathroom with a curbless shower and grab bars runs $15,000–$35,000, while converting a first-floor room into a full primary suite with an accessible bath can run $60,000–$90,000+. We prioritize the changes with the biggest safety impact first.
Universal design creates spaces usable by people of all ages and abilities without looking like medical modifications. Think curbless showers, lever handles, comfort-height counters, and wider doorways that benefit everyone — from young children to grandparents. Crestline’s CAPS-certified team designs these features to blend seamlessly into your home’s style.
Standard homeowners insurance and Medicare generally don’t cover home modifications, though some long-term care insurance policies, VA benefits, and Illinois assistance programs may help. We’re happy to provide detailed documentation for any program you’re applying through, and we can phase the work to fit your budget and timeline.
