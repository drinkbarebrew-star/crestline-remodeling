Decks & Patios

North Shore Decks & Patios You Can Count On

North Shore summers are short and precious, and a well-built deck or patio extends your living space into them. Crestline Remodeling designs and builds outdoor spaces engineered for the Chicago freeze-thaw cycle — proper footings, drainage, and materials that won’t heave, rot, or fade.

From low-maintenance composite decks to full paver patios with outdoor kitchens and fire features, we build outdoor rooms that connect seamlessly to the house and hold up to decades of Midwest weather.

Quick stat: 42 in. — frost-depth footings, built to code.

What’s Included

  • Composite and hardwood decking
  • Paver and natural stone patios
  • Outdoor kitchens and built-in grills
  • Fire pits, fireplaces, and seat walls
  • Pergolas, pavilions, and shade structures
  • Frost-depth footings and integrated drainage

What Does Decks & Patios Cost in Highland Park?

A composite deck typically runs $18,000–$45,000; a full paver patio with outdoor kitchen and fire feature commonly runs $50,000–$120,000+. Every project is different, so Crestline provides a fixed-price proposal after the design phase — no vague ranges, no surprise change orders.

Typical investment: $15,000 – $120,000 for decks & patios on the North Shore.

Our Process

  1. Free consultation — we visit your home, listen to your goals, and discuss a realistic budget range.
  2. Design & engineering — floor plans, 3D renderings, and material selections with real-time pricing.
  3. Fixed-price proposal — a detailed, line-item contract you approve before any work begins.

Decks & Patios FAQs

Common questions about deck and patio construction on Chicago’s North Shore.

Yes — most decks and many patios require permits, and North Shore municipalities enforce setback, height, and railing codes strictly. Decks over 30 inches off grade have specific railing and footing requirements. Crestline pulls all permits and builds to code, including the 42-inch frost-depth footings Illinois requires to prevent heaving.
Composite decking lasts 25–30 years with virtually no maintenance, while pressure-treated wood lasts 10–15 years and needs annual sealing. Premium hardwoods like Ipe can last 40+ years but require upkeep. For most North Shore homeowners, composite offers the best balance of longevity and low maintenance against our harsh freeze-thaw cycles.
